3 The modern inmensity of the urban

In pre-modern times, we had a relationship with the urban environment that could be addressed, if not manipulated, through locomotion. Wandering was a sufficient mode of transport to experience and intuitively assimilate the urban tissue. The size of the city could be classified, using Freundschuh and Egenhofer’s spaces, as an environmental scale, where the designer could become subject to the different perceptual cues of the city over time

As cities grew larger, their scale began to represent not just a practical complication of transport, but an ontological withdrawal of the urban phenomenon and an epistemological shift toward more abstract ways of conceiving the city. The technological development of transport—cars, trains, and planes— allowed us to transformed the experience of citizens into a discontinuous transit between environments. Although this technological development allowed us to handle and tailor our own individual urban environments within the city, we lost an general aspect of the urban. The passage to a geographic scale meant that we were cognitively surpassed by the urban information (Stolk & Portugali, 2016).

Across the spam of two centuries this change in magnitude widrew the experience of the city from our sensibilities as we knew it. It transformed it into a metropolis, assembled in megalopolis and nested finaly in a ecumenopolis: The phenomena of the urban enviromented enters now partialy in our lifes, closer to climate and other worldly events, it has become psicolgicably increasingly inaccesible in its totality by pure sensible intuition, and the design medium trougth wich we concive them have turn virtual domains. What was lost during transformation? How do we did built the modern city if not trougth intuition? Who were technological “winners” and the “loser” of this revolution? Why intuition cannot be convey througt modern design medium?? How could we trace a genalogy of intuitive design mediums?
